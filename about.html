Edgar Cruz is a photo-based BIPOC artist who holds a B.A. in Fine Art and Environmental Studies from UC Santa Cruz. Through analog and alternative photography, Edgar explores his personal relationship to space and environment. His work utilizes monochromatic light and shadows to create narratives of loneliness, detachment, and human connection. He uses various photographic processes including silver-gelatin, cyanotypes, platinum/palladium, photopolymer gravure, copper etchings, and alcohol transfers. Edgar is currently living in his home neighborhood of South Central Los Angeles.

Prints are available for purchase.

I explore different geographic landscapes, examining myself in these spaces; but most importantly how I fit in these various environments. Through monochromatic light and shadows, I attempt to visualize emotions of loneliness, detachment, and disconnection. Self-portraits through shadows document my presence in different environments but also fails to reveal my connection to these places. This inward point of view allows me to reflect and think about how I occupy different places. Multiple exposures allow me to create my own world to inhabit space within. What my eyes see is not something that anyone else will ever see. This results in an outward interpretation of the world that only exists as a metaphysical reality. The duality of my inward self-portraits and outward landscapes are equally important in examining how I connect with the rest of the world. My abstracted landscapes don’t attempt to depict reality but instead attempt to explore the relationships humans have to land. I hope to continue exploring these relationships, further challenging the ideas of photography; as a means of documentation and truth.
